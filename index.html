Research

My research focuses mainly on demographic modeling and reconstruction of evolutionary dynamics. I am part of the Ghirotto Population Genomics Lab at the University of Ferrara. My PhD project focuses on the study of ancient human populations in Italy analysing ancient whole-genome sequences thorugh different approaches (such as Genotype Likelihood and Imputation frameworks). I am also collaborating on projects involving different fields, such as bacterial markers for tracing human migrations and the reconstruction of demographic dynamics in invasive species.
My main topics of interest are: Population Genomics, Ancient DNA, Bioinformatics and Evolutionary Biology.
