Rajiv Boscolo Agostini


PhD student in Evolutionary Biology and Ecology

University of Ferrara

My research focuses mainly on demographic modeling and reconstruction of evolutionary dynamics.
I am currently studying human population dynamics in prehistoric Italy.
I am also working on other subjects, such as invasion dynamics in H. halys and bacterial markers for human migrations.
My main topics of interest are: Population Genomics, Bioinformatics and Evolutionary Biology.

PhD Project

The evolutionary history of Italy from Mesolithic to the present: lessons from ancient genomes

I am investigating the genetic variation and demographic dynamics in ancient population in the Italian peninsula and in the nearby islands of Sicily, Sardinia and Corsica, employing low-coverage whole genomes and genetic markers (SNPs, mtDNA, Y-chromosome).
This research is part of the projects:
“1000 Ancient Italian Genomes: Evidence from ancient biomolecules for unravelling past human population Dynamics (AGED)” coordinated by the University of Florence (20177PJ9XF).
“Crossing the Sea: ancient and modern human genomes to study the evolutionary dynamics of Sicily, Sardinia and Corsica” coordinated by the University of Ferrara (2020TACEZR).

Side Projects

Brown Marmorated Stinkbug invasion dynamics


I am investigating the invasion dynamics of the highly invasive brown marmorated stinkbug (H. halys) employing published ddRAD data and an inferential ABC framework.

Helicobacter pylori Genome Project (HpGP)


In collaboration with the Helicobacter pylori Genome Project (HpGP) Research network. I contributed to the analysis of the genomic structure in newly produced H. pylori genomes.
